## Remember to Tell Those Folks "Thank You"
Another fine afternoon, this time in [[Locations/Brugatiel\|Brugatiel]]. [[Characters/Brugatiel/Rodrique Khil\|Rod]] wanders the streets of Rominter alongside his constant companion [[Characters/Brugatiel/Brank O. Fjord\|Brank]]. His current goal is following the trail of a delicious smell.

The pair comes upon a clearing in the trees - a plaza where the forest floor is replaced with paving stones. There is a large banquet going on here, with high quality food filling a huge table and seats filled with members of Rominter's noble families. At the head of the table is
a man who looks much grander than the others. Though they fail to recognize him, this is the [[Characters/Brugatiel/Minor Brugatielans\|prince]] of Brugatiel.

In an authoritative voice, the prince announces to the gathered nobles that he offers them this gracious bounty in exchange for their continued support. Lacking focus due to the presence of so much delicious food, Rod comes to the conclusion that the offer includes him as well. Declaring "please" to a noblewoman, he happily snatches her steak and adds "thank you" before walking away. Brank has taught him well.

Unfortunately, the nobles do not appreciate his manners. An argument breaks out, but Brank is able to smooth things over and mostly calm them down. The prince agrees to let this slide for now, as long as they get going immediately. However, he strongly implies that he will take it out on Rod later. Before leaving, Rod takes a bit more food for the road (including the prince's meal) and "pays" for it by leaving a crumpled, mostly empty bag of potato-based snacks. While Brank distracts the nobles, Rod makes a clean getaway without further incident. He has yet to understand the proper value of things, but Brank has taught him well.

As they leave the party, Rod eats his food and makes a mess of his clothes. Brank wants him to wear something nicer anyways, and so intends to get new clothes tailored for him. Rod reluctantly agrees, but refuses to have his coat replaced (or washed). Brank then sets their new destination as [[Characters/Brugatiel/Minor Brugatielans\|Rena]]'s tailor shop, in the market district of Rominter.

On the way there, they encounter a strange dog-like creature that smells like fabric. It is scarred in many places, with wads of fluff coming out of one of the wounds. Since it isn't moving at all, wonder if it is even alive, and it suddenly begins to move. This is their first encounter with a stuffle, and the creature is called [[Characters/Brugatiel/Minor Brugatielans\|Rugged Ebony]].

He explains his origins, and that he had to move away from [[Locations/Popolala\|Popolala]] due to his allergy to rainbows. He also explains that his many scars came from his attempt at life as an adventurer, and lifts his eyepatch to show that one of his eyes is missing. Mistaking it for some kind of fashionable headband, Rod offers to trade the eyepatch for something from his button collection - in true stuffed animal fashion, Rugged Ebony's remaining eye resembles an ordinary button.

He gratefully accepts the trade, and Brank sews the shiny new button onto his face, as well as stitching up any remaining wounds. When Rugged Ebony explains that stuffles aren't meant for the front lines, Rod misunderstands and tells him that he should try drawing other lines instead. This inspires Rugged Ebony to take up a new life as an artist.

Afterwards, they finally arrive at Rena's. Brank asks her to create a new coat for Rod, one that is waterproof so that Rod will allow it to be cleaned. She agrees to make this "raincoat", and tells them to return in a couple of days to pick it up. They begin to leave, and encounter a huge crowd of Rod's fans outside in the marketplace. Brank picks up Rod and runs away, and the paparazzi chase begins.

Brank's sudden surge of strength and speed carries him a good distance away. At some point, he detects something strange with the ground and steps aside - and the villagers chasing them tumble forward into a pitfall trap. Brank overhears the prince's voice from deeper in the trees, cursing this turn of events and calling Rod an imposter. It seems he may have been responsible for this trap.

Nevertheless, Brank is able to evade the crowd because of this. They eventually find a spot in the underbrush to hide out for a while. Suddenly, the prince appears and shoots Brank in the back with a crossbow. It doesn't hurt him, but he soon realizes that the bolt must have been poisoned.

Rod is quick to retaliate, sinking into the shadows of the trees and disappearing. He then emerges directly beside the prince and smacks the crossbow out of his hands with a rock-hard loaf of stale bread. He then whips up a hazelnut macchiato and tosses it to Brank, which clears up the poison.

The tide is starting to turn against him, but then the prince whistles for aid. Several of his cronies appear with crossbows of their own, then open fire on the pair. They are able to withstand the assault, then Rod tackles the prince and holds him hostage until the cronies back off.

Having seemingly secured their victory, they scurry away to find a new safe place to live. The nobles celebrate, as from their perspective, they have finally driven the "imposter" out of Brugatiel.
