## Why Don't Those Folks Show Some Respect?
High noon in Dodgestone, the capital city of [[Locations/Alamaste\|Alamaste]]. [[Characters/Alamaste/Ace Phoenix\|Ace Phoenix]] patrols town alone, and as always draws the eyes of the citizens. Some greet him enthusiastically while others keep their distance from him. It seems his presence elicits mixed reactions. Unusually, he is currently alone, without his large robotic companion [[Characters/Alamaste/En Jean\|En Jean]].

Suddenly, he is alerted to a disturbance by the nearby sound of shattering glass. The swinging doors to the Steel Pony saloon fly open, sending a man sprawling out into the street - narrowly missing Ace in the process. After a moment to get info from the man, Ace proceeds into the saloon.

People are fleeing as he enters. However, there is a group around a table, most of whom are sitting down. The one standing, a tough-looking man who later introduces himself as [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Storm Remington]], is being accused of cheating at a game of cards. He remains defiant in the face of Ace’s threats, refusing to “take it outside”. He is even pointing a firearm at one of the accusers. However when Ace reveals that he is armed, Remington’s tough facade crumbles and he shakily removes a handful of coins from his pocket. He claims that he just realized he somehow accidentally ended up with their money.

Immediately after returning the clearly stolen money, Remington attempts to bolt out of the saloon. But he is unable to get past Ace, who is blocking the door. The criminal gets slammed into the ground and detained by Ace. He is then promptly taken to the Law Depot, which includes holding cells for people like Storm Remington.

Here, Ace encounters the [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Marshal]] of Dodgestone, the man in charge of all of Alamaste. He can’t remember the marshal’s name, but he does hand over Remington - whose real name turns out to be Bart Sutton - to the marshal’s custody. The two lawmen have a conversation about a notorious outlaw named [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Hank Wyoming]], who once ran a criminal empire across Dodgestone and beyond. This was before Ace’s time, as Wyoming was deposed by [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Micah]] - the man who previously held the Archon title. Ace doesn’t seem to like previous Archons being mentioned. Regardless, it seems all sorts of criminals have been springing up to fill the void left by Wyoming.

Ace is eager to find his companion Jean, so the Marshal directs him to a business called Elbow Grease. This workshop performs maintenance and repairs on tools and machinery, and is run by [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Caroline]], an acquaintance of Ace’s. As the Marshal suspected, Jean is here conversing with her. Because they are friends, not (as Ace suspected) because Jean got broken and needs repairs. Ace avoids talking to Caroline as he retrieves Jean.

Ace resumes his patrol with Jean in tow, heading for the city center. Eventually, they hear someone causing a ruckus to the south - Jean assumes his motorcycle form, and Ace hops aboard to put a stop to it. Here he finds a masked man on horseback in the outskirts of Dodgestone. The horse’s movements are strange and jerky. As they approach it, they notice it is in fact a mechanical approximation of a horse, and not a particularly well-made one. His name is [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Spade]], and the furry little man accompanying him - and proclaiming to have built the horse himself - is [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Duster]].

The rider struggles to maintain control of it, but nevertheless manages to challenge Ace to a duel. They both dismount, and he demands that Ace not allow Jean to be part of the battle - it’s just between the two of them. After all, as Spade claims, they are rivals. But Ace easily wins the quickdraw. His shot grazes Spade's face and undoes his bandana, briefly revealing a face that is younger than Ace was expecting.

Spade is quick to go back on his word, ordering his other minion [[Characters/Alamaste/Minor Alamastans\|Tumble]] to attack Ace from behind. In the scuffle between the two groups, Ace realizes he has somehow lost his invincibility. Jean joins in the fight to help dispatch the hoodlums, making the fight a breeze.

However, the horse gets out of control and begins stampeding. While Ace is able to lasso it, he can't stop its charge. Jean returns to motorcycle form to help. Just as it is about to slam into a building, Ace finally manages to wrangle it to a halt. Jean powers it down. Ace requests that some of the spectators cart the janky horse off to Elbow Grease.

Ace decides he wants a drink after that whole mess, but upon approaching the Tungsten Stallion sees Caroline inside. He quickly changes his mind and leaves, but not before she catches sight of him. She follows him into an alleyway, where she questions him about why he looks shaken up.

Caroline becomes concerned when Ace admits that something is happening to his powers. She mentions that in spite of his shortcomings, he has powers beyond what any previous Archon has been able to do, including Micah. Despite their complicated relationship, she is concerned not only for Alamaste's fate, but for him as well.

She doesn't know what's going on, but suggests that he appeal to the gods for help. This involves traveling outside of Alamaste, as the advances in technology has led Alamastans to become less interested in the divine over time. After drawing him a simple map that will lead him to [[Locations/Cepriso\|Cepriso]], she sends him out to look for a shrine or church.
